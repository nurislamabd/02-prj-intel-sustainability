Through the Ages

Five decades of innovation mapped against a single question: how does a company that makes the world faster also leave it cleaner?

1968 — 2024  ·  Nine milestones

1968
Intel Founded
1968 · Origin
Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation — and eventually, a commitment to build it all responsibly.

1971
First Microprocessor
1971 · Breakthrough
First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004 — the world's first commercial microprocessor. A 2,300-transistor chip that fit in your palm and ignited the revolution that would reshape every industry on earth.

1978
8086 Processor
1978 · Architecture
8086 Processor

The 8086 establishes the x86 architecture that still powers the majority of the world's PCs and servers today — a platform that would eventually demand a more sustainable manufacturing process at scale.

1985
386 Processor
1985 · Performance
386 Processor

The 32-bit 386 ushers in true multitasking for personal computers. With it came a new manufacturing scale — and the early seeds of awareness around the environmental cost of semiconductor production.

2006
Peak GHG Emissions
2006 · Turning Point
Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's greenhouse gas output reaches its all-time high. Rather than accept it as the cost of scale, Intel begins heavy investment in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient fabs — beginning a long, measurable reversal.

2020
RISE Strategy
2020 · Commitment
RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE — Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling — along with 2030 goals spanning climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction across its entire global supply chain.

2022
Net-Zero by 2040
2022 · Pledge
Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 & 2) across all global operations by 2040 — a landmark pledge grounded in years of measurable environmental progress and industry leadership.

2023
Renewable Electricity
2023 · Achievement
Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide — proving that semiconductor manufacturing at global scale can run almost entirely on clean power, drastically cutting carbon emissions.

2024
Sustainability Summit
2024 · Collaboration
Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing at planetary scale.

Our initiatives

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the semiconductor industry.

Water & waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. Intel also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.
